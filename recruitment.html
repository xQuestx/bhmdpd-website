Why Join Bar Harbor & Mount Desert Police Department?

Officer Formby, Officer Harrington, and Officer Burne participating in the Special Olympics Torch Run

Community Service: Special Olympics Torch Run

The Law Enforcement Torch Run® for Special Olympics is the movement's largest grass-roots fundraiser and public awareness vehicle. At its most basic level the Torch Run is an actual running event, in which officers and athletes run the Flame of Hope™ to the Opening Ceremonies of local Special Olympics competitions and State and National Games.

Annually, more than 85,000 dedicated, compassionate and volunteer law enforcement officers participate in the torch run throughout 35 nations, 12 Canadian provinces and 50 US states, raising over $500 Million since its inception in 1981.

Our 5-Step Application Process

Our comprehensive application process ensures we select the best candidates to serve our communities.

Application Steps

1

Submit Application

Complete the initial interest form, then submit the full application with all required documents.

2

Physical Fitness Test

Pass the Maine Criminal Justice Academy fitness standards including 1.5-mile run, push-ups, sit-ups, and flexibility test.

3

Background Investigation

A comprehensive check of your criminal, employment, and personal history.

4

Interview Process

Multiple interviews, including a panel interview and a final interview with the Chief of Police.

5

Conditional Offer & Exams

Receive a conditional offer, followed by medical, drug, and psychological screenings.

Basic Requirements

We're looking for dedicated individuals of good character. No college degree is required - we value commitment and integrity.

Essential Requirements

Age

At least 21 years old (or 20 with 60 college credits)

Education

High school diploma or GED equivalent

License

Valid Maine driver's license (or ability to obtain one)

Background

No felony convictions and of good moral character

Fitness

Must pass the MCJA physical fitness test

Skills

Strong communication & problem-solving abilities

Important Information for Applicants

MCJA Testing Required: All non-certified applicants must have already passed the Maine Criminal Justice Academy ALERT test and Physical Fitness Test before submitting a full application.

Lateral Transfers Welcome: Currently certified Maine law enforcement officers are encouraged to apply. We offer competitive incentives and an abbreviated hiring process for experienced officers.

Automatic Disqualifiers

Any felony conviction
Domestic violence conviction
Prohibitive drug use history
Dishonesty during application

Preferred Qualifications

Military experience
College degree
Prior law enforcement/corrections
Bilingual or multilingual skills
Volunteer/community service

Police Academy Training

New, non-certified officers attend the 18-week Basic Law Enforcement Training Program (BLETP) at the Maine Criminal Justice Academy.

What You'll Learn

The BLETP is an intensive, residential program that provides the essential skills for a career in law enforcement. We provide full support to ensure your success.

Maine Criminal Justice Academy training prepares officers for real-world situations

Training curriculum includes:

  • Criminal Law & Procedures
  • Emergency Vehicle Operations
  • Defensive Tactics
  • Firearms Training
  • Crisis Intervention
  • Community Policing
  • First Aid & CPR
  • Criminal Investigations
