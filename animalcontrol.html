Protecting Community Safety and Animal Welfare

Animal Control Overview

The Bar Harbor and Mount Desert Police Departments are committed to protecting both community safety and animal welfare through professional animal control services.

Our Mission

We strive to create a safe environment for both residents and animals through education, enforcement, and compassionate response. Our animal control services focus on public safety while ensuring humane treatment of all animals.

Community Safety Guidelines

  • Maintain control of your pet at all times
  • Ensure proper identification (tags, microchips)
  • Follow local leash laws and ordinances
  • Address any concerning behavior promptly
  • Report stray or aggressive animals immediately

Responsible Pet Ownership

  • Keep vaccinations current
  • Spay or neuter your pets
  • Provide adequate shelter and care
  • Clean up after your pet
  • Register and license as required

Pet Licensing Requirements

Licensing Requirements

Required: All dogs over six months must be licensed annually in Bar Harbor and Mount Desert.
  • Age Requirement: Dogs 6 months and older
  • Renewal: Annual licensing required
  • Documentation: Current rabies vaccination certificate
  • Fee: Varies by spay/neuter status

Required Documents

  • Current rabies vaccination certificate
  • Spay/neuter certificate (if applicable)
  • Previous year's license (for renewals)
